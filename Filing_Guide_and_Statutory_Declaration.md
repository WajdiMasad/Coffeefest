## PART 3: WHAT HAPPENS NEXT?

1. **Review Period:** The Registry of Joint Stock Companies typically reviews electronic incorporation submissions within **3 to 10 business days**.
2. **Certificate of Incorporation:** Once approved, the Registry will issue:
   - A **Certificate of Incorporation** bearing your officially assigned corporate number (e.g., *4389123 Nova Scotia Limited*).
   - Your **Nova Scotia Business Number (BN)** (issued in partnership with the Canada Revenue Agency).
3. **Immediate Post-Incorporation Steps:**
   - **ISC Register:** Set up your Register of Individuals with Significant Control. As sole shareholder, Micah must be listed as the ISC. Record her full name, date of birth, address, tax residence (Canada), the date of incorporation, and the description of control (100% of Class A Voting Shares). Keep this register at the registered office alongside the minute book.
   - **Minute Book:** Prepare an organizational minute book containing copies of the Memorandum, Articles, Certificate of Incorporation, initial director/officer resolutions, first share certificate, and the ISC Register.
4. **Opening a Bank Account:** Once you receive your Certificate of Incorporation and Business Number, you can take copies of these filed documents to any Canadian financial institution to open a corporate business bank account under the company's name.
5. **Adding a Partner (In a Few Months):**
   - Because we authorized an unlimited number of Class A and Class B shares, when you are ready to bring in a partner, you can:
     1. Draft a simple **Share Subscription Agreement** between the Company and the new partner.
     2. Convene a meeting of the Board of Directors (or sign a sole-director written resolution) approving the allotment and issuance of shares (e.g., Class A Voting Shares for an active partner, or Class B Non-Voting Shares for an investor) in exchange for their contribution (cash, assets, etc.).
     3. Issue a new **Share Certificate** signed by you, and update the Company's **Register of Members** (shareholder registry) and **ISC Register** kept at your registered office.
     4. If needed, pass a Special Resolution to authorize any borrowing or mortgaging (per Article 17 / Section 88(2) of the Act).
     5. If you or your partner eventually decide to part ways or adjust equity holdings, the Company can execute a share buyback or redemption. This is fully supported by the specific share-purchase powers we integrated directly into the Articles (Articles 8A and 15(f)), avoiding the need for complex future amendments.
