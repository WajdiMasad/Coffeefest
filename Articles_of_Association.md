## PRIVATE COMPANY RESTRICTIONS
*(Required for Private Issuer Status under Canadian Securities Laws)*

4. The Company is a private company, and accordingly:
   * (a) **Shareholder Limit:** The number of shareholders of the Company (exclusive of persons who are in the employment of the Company, and persons who having been formerly in the employment of the Company were, while in such employment, and have continued after the determination of such employment to be, shareholders of the Company) shall be limited to fifty (50), provided that where two or more persons hold one or more shares in the Company jointly, they shall, for the purpose of this Article, be treated as a single shareholder.
   * (b) **Transfer Restriction:** No share or shares in the capital of the Company shall be transferred without the prior express approval of the Board of Directors, which approval may be evidenced by a resolution of the Directors or by a written instrument signed by a majority of the Directors.
   * (c) **Public Prohibition:** Any invitation to the public to subscribe for any shares, debentures, or other securities of the Company is strictly prohibited.

## SHARES AND SHARE CAPITAL

5. **Allotment and Issuance:** Subject to the provisions of the Memorandum of Association and these Articles, the allotment and issuance of shares in the capital of the Company shall be under the absolute control of the Directors. The Directors may allot, issue, grant options over, or otherwise dispose of shares to such persons, at such times, for such consideration (whether in cash, property, or services), and on such terms and conditions as the Directors, by resolution, deem fit.
   
6. **No Par Value:** All shares of the Company shall be without nominal or par value, and the consideration for the issuance of such shares shall be fixed by a resolution of the Directors.

7. **Payment of Shares:** A share shall not be issued until the consideration for the share is fully paid in money, property, or past services.

8. **Trusts Not Recognized:** Except as required by law, no person shall be recognized by the Company as holding any share upon any trust, and the Company shall not be bound by or compelled in any way to recognize any equitable, contingent, future, or partial interest in any share, or any other right in respect of any share, except an absolute right to the entirety thereof in the registered holder.

8A. **Purchase and Redemption of Own Shares:** Subject to the Act, the Company may, by a resolution of the Directors, purchase, redeem, or otherwise acquire any of its own issued shares of any class, out of any funds or capital properly available for such purpose, on such terms and in such manner as the Directors may from time to time determine. Any shares so purchased, redeemed, or acquired may be cancelled or held as treasury shares, or reissued, in accordance with the Act.

## SHARE CERTIFICATES

9. **Entitlement:** Every shareholder shall be entitled, without payment, to one certificate under the common seal of the Company (or signed by the authorized signing officers of the Company) specifying the share or shares held by him and the amount paid up thereon. If a share certificate is defaced, lost, or destroyed, it may be renewed on payment of such fee, if any, and on such terms, if any, as to evidence and indemnity as the Directors think fit.

10. **Signatures on Certificates:** Share certificates shall be signed by the President or a Director, and countersigned by the Secretary or an Assistant Secretary. If only one person holds the offices of Director, President, and Secretary, that person's single signature on the certificate shall be sufficient.

## TRANSFER AND TRANSMISSION OF SHARES

11. **Execution of Transfer:** The instrument of transfer of any share in the Company shall be in writing and shall be executed by both the transferor and the transferee, and the transferor shall be deemed to remain the holder of the share until the name of the transferee is entered in the Register in respect thereof.

12. **Form of Transfer:** The instrument of transfer of shares shall be in the following form, or in any other form which the Directors may approve:

    > **SHARE TRANSFER INSTRUMENT**
    >
    > For value received, I, **[TRANSFEROR NAME]**, hereby transfer unto **[TRANSFEREE NAME]** of **[TRANSFEREE ADDRESS]**, **[NUMBER]** **[CLASS]** Shares in the capital of **[NUMBER] Nova Scotia Limited**, to hold unto the transferee, their executors, administrators, and assigns, subject to the conditions on which I held the same at the time of the execution hereof; and I, the transferee, hereby agree to take the said shares subject to the conditions aforesaid.
    >
    > **Dated** this ______ day of ________________, 20___.
    >
    > Witness to Transferor: ____________________ | Transferor: ____________________
    >
    > Witness to Transferee: ____________________ | Transferee: ____________________

13. **Directors' Absolute Right to Decline:** The Directors may, in their absolute and unfettered discretion and without assigning any reason therefor, decline to register any transfer of any share, whether or not it is a fully paid share. This power is paramount, particularly to preserve the private nature of the Company or protect the shareholder structure. If the Directors refuse to register a transfer, they shall send to the transferee notice of the refusal within thirty (30) days after the date on which the transfer was lodged with the Company.

14. **Transmission on Death:** In the case of the death of a shareholder, the survivors or survivor where the deceased was a joint holder, and the legal personal representatives of the deceased where he was a sole holder, shall be the only persons recognized by the Company as having any title to his shares.

## ALTERATION OF CAPITAL

15. **Powers of Alteration:** The Company may, from time to time, by Special Resolution:
    * (a) Increase its share capital by such number of shares as the resolution shall prescribe;
    * (b) Consolidate and divide all or any of its share capital into shares of larger amount than its existing shares;
    * (c) Sub-divide its shares, or any of them, into shares of smaller amount than is fixed by the Memorandum of Association;
    * (d) Cancel shares which, at the date of the passing of the resolution, have not been taken or agreed to be taken by any person;
    * (e) Reduce its share capital in any manner authorized by the Act; and
    * (f) Purchase, redeem, or otherwise acquire any of its own issued shares in any manner authorized by the Act.

## BORROWING POWERS

16. **Directors' Authority:** Subject to the provisions of Section 88 of the Act:
    * (a) The Directors may, from time to time, at their discretion, borrow or raise money for the purposes of the Company;
    * (b) The Directors may secure the payment of any such money, or any other debt, liability, or obligation of the Company, in such manner and upon such terms and conditions as they think fit, including by pledge, charge, or assignment of all or any part of the property, assets, and undertaking of the Company (both present and future), including its uncalled capital;
    * (c) The Directors may issue debentures, debenture stock, bonds, and other securities, whether outright or as security for any debt, liability, or obligation of the Company or of any third party.

17. **Special Resolution Required for Mortgages and Debentures:** In accordance with Section 88(2) of the Act, the power to execute mortgages of the real or personal property of the Company and the power to issue debentures (whether secured by mortgage or otherwise) shall **not** be exercised by the Directors except with the **sanction of a Special Resolution** of the Company passed at a general meeting of which notice specifying the intention to propose the resolution has been duly given. The Special Resolution so passed shall be filed with the Registrar of Joint Stock Companies within fifteen (15) days of its passing.

## MEETINGS OF SHAREHOLDERS

18. **Annual General Meeting:** The Annual General Meeting of the Company shall be held at such time and place as the Directors shall determine, in accordance with the provisions of the Act.

19. **Special General Meetings:** All general meetings of the shareholders other than Annual General Meetings shall be called Special General Meetings. The Directors may call a Special General Meeting whenever they think fit.

20. **Notice of Meetings:** At least fourteen (14) clear days' notice (exclusive of the day on which the notice is served or deemed to be served, but inclusive of the day for which notice is given) specifying the place, day, and hour of the meeting, and in case of special business, the general nature of such business, shall be given to the shareholders in the manner hereinafter mentioned. Where a Special Resolution is to be proposed, the notice shall specify the intention to propose the resolution as a Special Resolution.
    * **Waiver of Notice:** A meeting of shareholders may be held at any time and place without notice if all the shareholders entitled to vote thereat are present in person or by proxy, or if those not present waive notice in writing either before or after the meeting.

21. **Electronic/Virtual Meetings:** Any shareholder or other person entitled to attend a meeting of shareholders may participate in the meeting by means of telephonic, electronic, or other communication facility that permits all participants to communicate adequately with each other during the meeting, if the Company makes such a facility available. A person participating in a meeting by such means is deemed to be present in person at the meeting.

## PROCEEDINGS AT SHAREHOLDER MEETINGS

22. **Quorum:** No business shall be transacted at any meeting of shareholders unless a quorum is present at the time when the meeting proceeds to business. 
    * **Quorum Rule:** Two (2) persons present in person or by proxy and holding or representing by proxy a majority of the issued shares of the Company entitled to vote at the meeting shall constitute a quorum.
    * **Sole Shareholder Provision:** **Notwithstanding the above, if and so long as the Company has only one (1) shareholder, that one shareholder present in person or by proxy shall constitute a quorum for all purposes of a meeting of shareholders.**

23. **Adjournment:** If within half an hour from the time appointed for the meeting a quorum is not present, the meeting, if convened upon the requisition of shareholders, shall be dissolved; in any other case, it shall stand adjourned to the same day in the next week, at the same time and place, and if at the adjourned meeting a quorum is not present within half an hour from the time appointed for the meeting, the shareholders present shall be a quorum.

24. **Chairman:** The President of the Company, or in his absence, a Vice-President, shall preside as Chairman at every meeting of shareholders. If there is no such Chairman, or if at any meeting he is not present within fifteen minutes after the time appointed for holding the meeting, the shareholders present shall choose one of their number to be Chairman.

25. **Voting:** At any meeting of shareholders, a resolution put to the vote of the meeting shall be decided on a show of hands unless a poll is demanded. Every shareholder present in person or by proxy who holds a voting share shall have one vote on a show of hands, and upon a poll, shall have one vote for each voting share held by him.
    * **Casting Vote:** In the case of an equality of votes, whether on a show of hands or on a poll, the Chairman of the meeting shall have a second or casting vote.

26. **Proxies:** A shareholder entitled to attend and vote at a meeting of shareholders may, by instrument in writing, appoint a proxyholder to attend and act at the meeting in the manner and to the extent authorized by the proxy. A proxy must be deposited with the Company not less than forty-eight (48) hours before the time for holding the meeting at which the person named in the proxy proposes to vote.
    * **Form of Proxy:** An instrument appointing a proxy may be in the following form, or in any other form which the Directors may approve:
    
        > **INSTRUMENT OF PROXY**
        >
        > I, **[SHAREHOLDER NAME]**, being a shareholder of **[NUMBER] Nova Scotia Limited** (the "Company"), hereby appoint **[PROXYHOLDER NAME]** of **[PROXYHOLDER ADDRESS]** (or failing him, **[ALTERNATIVE PROXYHOLDER NAME]**) as my proxy to attend, vote, and act for me and on my behalf at the general meeting of the shareholders of the Company to be held on the ______ day of ________________, 20___, and at any adjournment or adjournments thereof.
        >
        > **Dated** this ______ day of ________________, 20___.
        >
        > ____________________________________
        > Signature of Shareholder

27. **Written Resolutions:** A resolution in writing signed by all the shareholders of the Company entitled to vote on that resolution at a meeting of shareholders is as valid as if it had been passed at a meeting of the shareholders, and shall be deemed to date from the day it was signed by the last shareholder.

## BOARD OF DIRECTORS

28. **Number:** The number of Directors of the Company shall not be less than one (1) nor more than ten (10), as determined from time to time by an ordinary resolution of the shareholders.
    * **First Director:** The first Director of the Company shall be **MICAH ADRIENNE LACSON**.

29. **Qualification:** A Director is not required to hold any shares in the Company to qualify for office.

30. **Removal of Directors:** The shareholders may, by ordinary resolution passed at a Special General Meeting called for that purpose, remove any Director before the expiration of his period of office, and may, by ordinary resolution, appoint another person in his stead.

31. **Casual Vacancies:** Any casual vacancy occurring on the Board of Directors may be filled by the remaining Directors, but the person so chosen shall hold office only until the next Annual General Meeting.

## POWERS AND DUTIES OF DIRECTORS

32. **General Powers:** The business and affairs of the Company shall be managed by the Directors, who may exercise all such powers of the Company and do all such lawful acts and things as are not by the Act, the Memorandum of Association, or by these Articles, required to be exercised or done by the shareholders in general meeting.

33. **Appointment of Officers:** The Directors shall from time to time appoint a President, a Secretary, and such other officers (including a Treasurer, Vice-Presidents, and Assistant Officers) as they deem necessary, and may define their duties, powers, and terms of employment.

## PROCEEDINGS OF DIRECTORS

34. **Board Meetings:** The Directors may meet together for the dispatch of business, adjourn, and otherwise regulate their meetings and proceedings as they think fit. A Director may at any time summon a meeting of the Directors.

35. **Notice:** Notice of a meeting of Directors shall be given to each Director not less than forty-eight (48) hours before the time when the meeting is to be held, provided that a meeting of Directors may be held at any time without notice if all the Directors are present, or if those absent waive notice in writing either before or after the meeting.

36. **Quorum:** The quorum necessary for the transaction of the business of the Directors may be fixed by the Directors, and unless so fixed shall be two (2) Directors.
    * **Sole Director Provision:** **Notwithstanding the above, if and so long as there is only one (1) Director in office, that sole Director shall constitute a quorum for all meetings of Directors, and all decisions of the Board may be validly made by a single written resolution signed by such sole Director.**

37. **Voting:** Questions arising at any meeting of Directors shall be decided by a majority of votes. In case of an equality of votes, the Chairman shall have a second or casting vote.

38. **Written Resolutions:** A resolution in writing signed by all the Directors entitled to receive notice of a meeting of Directors shall be as valid and effectual as if it had been passed at a meeting of Directors duly called and constituted.

39. **Electronic Participation:** A Director may participate in a meeting of Directors by means of telephonic, electronic, or other communication facility that permits all participants to communicate adequately with each other. A Director participating in a meeting by such means is deemed to be present in person at the meeting.

## DIVIDENDS AND RESERVES

48. **Declaration:** The Directors may from time to time declare dividends payable to the shareholders according to their respective rights and interests in the Company, out of profits or funds properly available for distribution. 
    * **Class Discretion:** The Directors may, in their absolute discretion, declare a dividend on one class of common shares (e.g., Class A Common Shares) to the complete exclusion of any other class of common shares (e.g., Class B Common Shares), or vice-versa.

49. **Interim Dividends:** The Directors may from time to time pay to the shareholders such interim dividends as appear to the Directors to be justified by the profits of the Company.

50. **No Interest:** No dividend shall bear interest against the Company.

## ACCOUNTS, RECORDS, AND NOTICES

51. **Books of Account:** The Directors shall cause true accounts to be kept of all receipts and expenditures, assets and liabilities, and all other transactions of the Company. The books of account and other corporate records shall be kept at the registered office of the Company or at such other place in Nova Scotia as the Directors think fit, and shall be open to inspection by the Directors at all times.

52. **Register of Individuals with Significant Control:** In accordance with the Act (as amended effective April 1, 2023), the Company shall create and maintain a Register of Individuals with Significant Control ("ISC Register") as part of its corporate records. The ISC Register shall contain, for each individual who has significant control over the Company (as defined in the Act), the following information:
    * (a) The individual's full legal name and date of birth;
    * (b) The individual's last known residential address;
    * (c) The jurisdiction of residence of the individual for tax purposes;
    * (d) The date on which the individual became or ceased to be an individual with significant control;
    * (e) A description of how the individual has significant control, including a description of their interests and rights in shares of the Company; and
    * (f) A description of the steps taken by the Company in each financial year to ensure the accuracy and completeness of the ISC Register.
    
    The Directors shall take reasonable steps at least once in each financial year to identify all individuals with significant control and to ensure the ISC Register is accurate, complete, and up to date. The ISC Register shall be updated within fifteen (15) days of the Company becoming aware of any change.

53. **Service of Notice:** A notice or other document may be served by the Company upon any shareholder either personally, by sending it through the post in a prepaid envelope addressed to such shareholder at his registered address, or by sending it via electronic mail (email) to the email address provided by the shareholder.
